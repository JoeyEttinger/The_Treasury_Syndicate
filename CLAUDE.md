# The Treasury Syndicate — CLAUDE.md

## Project Overview

The Treasury Syndicate is a Decentralized Autonomous Organization (DAO) built to crowdfund and execute coordinated marketing and influence operations on behalf of Bitcoin treasury companies (e.g. Strategy/MSTR, Strive/ASST). It operates as a rogue intelligence and special-ops unit — not affiliated with or sanctioned by those companies, but acting in their interest as passionate shareholders.

The aesthetic and positioning is deliberate: this is not an education platform or a podcast network. It is a synchronized, capital-backed operations unit.

---

## The Problem

Bitcoin treasury companies have a structural marketing constraint: every dollar spent on marketing is one less dollar spent on Bitcoin. This creates an ideological and financial tension that means these companies are chronically under-marketed despite representing a genuinely revolutionary shift in corporate treasury management.

Shareholders want visibility and awareness to grow, but the companies themselves cannot prioritize it without compromising their core mission.

---

## The Solution

The Treasury Syndicate fills that gap by crowdsourcing capital from shareholders and deploying it toward coordinated operations — media campaigns, shareholder presence at investor days, counter-narrative ops, targeted allocator outreach, and more.

The DAO is governed by token holders who vote on proposals. Approved proposals are funded from the treasury and executed by a vetted group of intelligence leaders.

---

## Governance Model

- **Token Type:** Purchasable governance tokens (plutocratic model)
- **Voting Power:** 1 token = 1 permanent vote
- **Token Distribution:** Tokens are purchased directly through the platform via Privy wallets
- **Proposal Flow:** Any member can submit an operation proposal with a budget recommendation → token holders vote → approved proposals are funded from treasury → intelligence leaders execute
- **No token transfers** beyond initial purchase (soulbound-style permanence)

---

## Monetization

- **Developer cut:** A percentage of all raised capital goes to the founding development team, baked into the smart contract
- **Intelligence leader cut:** A separate percentage of raised capital or per-proposal fee goes to the operators who execute approved ops
- Both are enforced at the smart contract level — no discretionary allocation

### Why Stacks?
Stacks is a Bitcoin Layer 2 that settles to the Bitcoin network. This is a deliberate narrative choice — the governance and treasury of a DAO that exists to serve Bitcoin treasury companies is itself secured by Bitcoin. It's thematically coherent and defensible to the target audience.

### Why Privy?
The target contributors are shareholders of MSTR, STRV, and similar companies — many of whom are not crypto-native. Privy enables seamless wallet creation and management without requiring users to understand seed phrases or self-custody, dramatically lowering the onboarding barrier.

### Why Guild?
Guild handles wallet-based role verification for Discord, allowing the DAO to gate channels and permissions based on verified on-chain token holdings.

## Operations (What the DAO Actually Does)

Operations ("ops") are the funded campaigns voted on and executed by the DAO. Examples include:

- Coordinated shareholder presence and Q&A at earnings calls and investor days
- Intelligence reports on Bitcoin treasury thesis distributed to financial media
- Targeted outreach campaigns to institutional allocators, pension funds, and RIAs
- Counter-narrative operations when mainstream media misrepresents the treasury model
- Synchronized social campaigns timed to catalysts (earnings, BTC price milestones, new company announcements)

Operations are proposed by members, scoped with a budget, voted on via the smart contract, and executed by intelligence leaders drawn from the operator pool.

---

## Key Roles

**Token Holders** — Buy tokens, vote on proposals, fund the treasury passively through their initial purchase.

**Intelligence Leaders** — Vetted operators who execute approved ops. Earn a cut of raised capital or per-op fees. These are the most powerful long-term participants in the DAO.

**Development Team** — Builds and maintains the smart contracts, frontend, and infrastructure. Takes a protocol-level percentage cut of raised capital.

## Legal Structure

The project intends to wrap under a **Marshall DAO LLC** (or equivalent DAO LLC structure) for operational liability protection. Note: full securities law review is required before launch given the pooled capital model and its proximity to coordinated activity around publicly traded securities.
